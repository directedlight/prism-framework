P - Process Complexity

Evaluating whether your workflow is complex enough to benefit from MCP implementation.

1. How many distinct expert domains are involved in your workflow? ?

3. What level of decision-making complexity is involved? ?

R - Resource Requirements

Assessing the technical infrastructure and budget needed for MCP implementation.

1. What API infrastructure do you currently have available? ?
Consider your current API ecosystem and how accessible your data and services are.
2. What is your budget allocation for AI implementation? ?
Consider both initial implementation costs and ongoing operational expenses.
3. What technical expertise is available in-house? ?
Consider your team's experience with AI systems and automation platforms.

I - Integration Potential

Evaluating how well MCP would connect with your existing systems.

1. How modular are your current systems and processes? ?
Consider whether your systems are built as independent services or as monolithic applications.
2. What data standardization exists across your systems? ?
Consider how consistently data is structured across different systems and departments.
3. How critical is unstructured data processing to your workflow? ?
Consider how much of your workflow involves analyzing and interpreting unstructured content.

S - Strategic Alignment

Determining if MCP aligns with your business goals and future vision.

1. How critical is process acceleration to your business strategy? ?
Consider the strategic value of reducing cycle times in your key processes.
2. How aligned is your organization with AI adoption? ?
Consider executive support, staff readiness, and cultural factors that might affect adoption.
3. What is your timeline for implementation and results? ?
Consider your expectations for when you need to see business impact.

M - Measurable Impact

Projecting the potential ROI and business impact.

1. How quantifiable are your current process inefficiencies? ?
Consider whether you have metrics for current process performance and where improvements are needed.
2. How much variation exists in your process execution? ?
Consider how much your processes vary from one execution to the next.
3. What risk mitigation capability is needed? ?
Consider the level of verification and oversight needed in your processes.
